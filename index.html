Agentic maths assessment · v0.3

Every learner has
hidden mines.

Most assessments find the wrong mine. They catch what a student got wrong, not why, and miss entirely what's underneath: the fixed mindset that quits at the first wall, the exam anxiety that blanks under pressure, the resilience (or absence of it) that shapes every result. Mindsweeper runs six specialised AI agents in parallel to sweep the complete terrain: mapping knowledge strand by strand, and profiling the learner's full mathematical character: mindset, motivation, affect, and resilience. What emerges is not a score. It's a map of how a student thinks, feels, and fights.

1
Knowledge & Skill
Assessor
Guideline
Classify every response against the target CCSS/IGCSE/IB strand. Tag as procedural (can execute) or conceptual (understands why). Log errors by type: careless slip, procedural bug, conceptual gap, or transfer failure.
Rationale
Bloom's taxonomy separates remembering/applying from understanding/analysing. A student can execute percentage change correctly without understanding multiplicative structure. The two decay independently and need separate intervention.
Data reasoning
Root-gap analysis uses the SAP Coherence Map prerequisite graph. The node with the highest downstream dependency count (fan-out) is the highest-leverage fix. All CCSS standard codes verified live via Learning Commons MCP before being written to the knowledge map.
Learning Commons · SAP Coherence Map · ANet decomposition
2
Mindset & Motivation
Assessor
Guideline
Listen for attribution language, effort framing, and goal orientation. Classify motivation along Ryan & Deci's SDT autonomy continuum: amotivation ("I don't see the point") → external regulation ("my parents make me") → introjected regulation ("I'll feel bad if I fail") → identified regulation ("maths matters for my future") → integrated regulation ("being good at maths is part of who I am") → intrinsic motivation ("I genuinely enjoy this"). Separately track Dweck growth vs fixed mindset.
Rationale
Ryan & Deci (2000) Self-Determination Theory — the intrinsic/extrinsic binary is too coarse for intervention. Introjected regulation (shame-driven) and identified regulation (goal-driven) both appear "extrinsic" on a simple scale but require opposite scaffolding: the first needs self-compassion work; the second needs challenge and stretch. Dweck (2006) growth mindset operates as an orthogonal axis — a student can have identified regulation and a fixed mindset simultaneously.
Data reasoning
SDT classification from dialogue cues: shame/pride language → introjected. Parental/grade pressure → external. Future-goal language → identified. Curiosity, enjoyment, unprompted exploration → intrinsic. Amotivation flagged when engagement drops below threshold across 3 consecutive probes. Conscientiousness inferred from working completeness and self-monitoring frequency in the portfolio.
3
Resilience & Affect
Assessor
Guideline
Track behaviour under perturbation: when the probe gets harder, does the student revise, abandon, catastrophise, or persist? Distinguish cliff (sudden collapse) from graceful slope (gradual degradation). Flag exam anxiety markers: excessive revision of correct answers, blank responses on known material.
Rationale
Yeager & Dweck (2012) on stress-is-enhancing vs stress-is-debilitating beliefs. Anxiety narrows working memory, directly degrading procedural execution on multi-step problems. Dweck & Leggett (1988) on performance vs mastery goals under difficulty.
Data reasoning
Cliff detection: performance drop >40% when difficulty increases one grade band. Anxiety signal: answer revision rate >30% on items the student got right first. Neuroticism inferred from revision frequency, self-deprecating language, and hesitation patterns.
4
Metacognition & Comms
Assessor
Guideline
After each probe, compare the student's predicted confidence with actual performance. Measure explanation quality when asked to teach back: does the explanation reveal understanding or mask it with procedure recitation?
Rationale
Flavell (1979) metacognitive monitoring. Calibration gap predicts help-seeking: overconfident students don't seek help; underconfident students don't attempt. Chi & Wylie (2014) ICAP framework: students who generate explanations retain more than passive receivers.
Data reasoning
Calibration gap = |predicted score − actual score| averaged across items. Gap >25% is high-risk. Explanation scored on: uses own words, gives an example, identifies the key concept, spots where errors would occur. Zero on any of these flags a surface learner.
5
Portfolio Ingestion
Evidence layer
Guideline
Convert uploaded PDFs/images into structured evidence objects. Extract: working steps, final answers, corrections and crossings-out, margin notes, and layout signals (working abandoned mid-page). Tag each element by question reference where discernible.
Rationale
Raw OCR noise misleads the Knowledge assessor. A crossing-out is not an error — it may be self-correction, which is a resilience signal. Layout and working completeness are evidence for Metacognition and Resilience, not only for Knowledge.
Data reasoning
Output schema: {question_ref, steps[], final_answer, corrections[], layout_notes, ocr_confidence}. OCR confidence below 0.7 triggers a low-reliability flag; the Orchestrator down-weights that evidence accordingly.
6
Orchestrator
Conductor
Guideline
At each turn, read the open-gaps list from all four assessors. Prioritise the gap with the highest cross-assessor uncertainty and highest downstream leverage. Select probe type: Reasoning Transparency, Perturbation, Teaching, or Calibration.
Rationale
The Orchestrator is the system's attention mechanism. It prevents over-indexing on knowledge (the default trap of any maths assessment) at the cost of character. If Resilience has high uncertainty after 6 exchanges, a perturbation probe is injected regardless of what Knowledge wants.
Data reasoning
OCEAN derived by synthesising all four assessors' logged behavioural primitives: openness (novelty-response), conscientiousness (working completeness), neuroticism (anxiety signals), agreeableness and extraversion (explanation style). No single assessor sees the full picture; the Orchestrator does.
7
Intervention Planner
Planner
Guideline
Read the completed profile and generate a sequenced plan across four dimensions: Knowledge (what to fix, in what order), Mindset (reframing strategies), Habit (study behaviour and metacognitive routines), Problem-solving (transfer and strategy flexibility). Each action must cite a learning-science rationale.
Rationale
Rosenshine (2012) Principles of Instruction: new learning sequences from prerequisite to dependent, practised with spaced retrieval, checked with formative assessment. Mindset and habit interventions follow Yeager (2022) wise interventions: small, targeted, belief-consistent actions outperform generic motivation pep talks. Strategy library drawn from LVN (Learning Variability Network) via Learning Commons.
Data reasoning
Knowledge intervention order = topological sort of the prerequisite graph, starting at the root gap (from Agent 1). Mindset type (from Agent 2) gates the reframing strategy. If calibration gap >25% (from Agent 4), metacognitive monitoring routines are added before content work. Strategy selection draws on Learning Commons' Achievement Network (ANet) learning component decomposition.
Learning Commons · LVN (Learning Variability Network) · ANet (Achievement Network) decomposition · Rosenshine (2012) · Yeager (2022)
