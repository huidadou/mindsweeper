LVNWorked Examples
Targets: Working Memory · Long-term Memory
Present fully worked examples first, then have the student attempt independently. LVN Math 7-10 indicates that error-example analysis is especially effective for conceptual development — present a flawed example (using 0.15 instead of 0.85) and have her identify and articulate where it went wrong.
Concrete actions: A 6-card mini-deck. Card 1: a + 0.05a = 1.05a (canonical 7.EE.A.2 example). Card 2: a − 0.15a = 0.85a (decay variant). Card 3: 18000 × 0.85³ (correct version of PT1-Q22b). Cards 4-6 introduce further variants. Gradually fade scaffolding: full solution → middle steps blank → final step blank → independent completion.
Research basis: Sweller (1988) cognitive load theory; Renkl (2014) worked example effect
LVNMath Talks
Targets: Math Communication · Long-term Memory · Self-efficacy
Have the student verbalise her mathematical reasoning. LVN Math Talks notes that articulating reasoning aloud consolidates long-term memory more reliably than silent practice.
Concrete actions: 15 minutes per session. Sample prompt: "Why is the multiplier for a 15% decrease 0.85 rather than 0.15? Explain it to me as if you were teaching a younger student." If she gets stuck, do not interrupt — wait for her to self-correct; that struggle is where learning happens.
Research basis: Chi & Wylie (2014) ICAP framework; Schoenfeld (1992)
LVNCognitive Offloading
Target: Working Memory
Explicitly teach her to use paper as external working memory. Tully et al. (2025) show that paper-based offloading yields the greatest benefit for students with prior knowledge in place — precisely Xiao Ting's profile.
Concrete actions: A three-step rule for every multi-step problem: (1) write the givens; (2) write the requirement; (3) state the method choice before computing. Apply this rule to every multi-step problem for two weeks. The goal is to automate external offloading before time pressure activates.
Research basis: Risko & Gilbert (2016); Tully et al. (2025) Br J Educ Psychol

§③ CONSOLIDATION GRADIENT

Consolidation gradient

Not all "knowing" is equivalent. Before designing interventions, we need to distinguish the maturity of the underlying conceptual representations.

Robust
Correct across ≥ 2 independent items and conditions, including under time pressure.
Emerging
Correct under standard conditions; no observed breakdown under pressure.
Recognised but unstable
The student can write the correct formula or method (conceptual representation is present), but execution is unstable. PT1-Q11 and PT1-Q22b fall in this band.
Introduced not internalised
Cannot map the problem onto the correct procedure; the conceptual structure has not yet formed. Bearings and similar solids fall in this band.

Working memory overload under non-calculator conditions Trainable · moderate
COGNITION · WORKING MEMORY · LEAD FACTOR
The core factor in this case and the strongest signal from the 12 March initial diagnostic: "Frequent sighing, sensitive to ambient noise; basic arithmetic and procedural execution consumed most of her cognitive resources, leaving insufficient capacity for logical reasoning." Per the Baddeley & Hitch working memory model, mental arithmetic under time pressure simultaneously occupies the central executive (decision-making), the phonological loop (verbal computation), and the visuospatial sketchpad (figure processing). When all three are loaded, the executive resource for self-monitoring is exhausted, producing the three observed error classes: (1) multi-step arithmetic collapse; (2) MCQ revisions (chose and changed); (3) mental computation leaving no written trace.

§⑨ METHODOLOGY

Sources, decision criteria, and limitations

The basis and boundaries of every judgement, transparent for peer review.

Knowledge Graph MCP query workflow
Each standard is resolved through three live API calls:
(1) find_standard_statement with the Multi-State jurisdiction. Key finding: high-school standards (Grades 9-12) require the HS prefix.
(2) find_learning_components_from_standard using the CASE network UUID. Key finding: the official ANet decomposition differs substantially from an intuitive partition. HSA-REI.B.4.b has 7 official LCs, and "method selection" is its own component.
(3) find_standards_progression_from_standard with forward/backward direction. Key correction: the v1 hypothesis that 8.EE.A.1 was the bottleneck was empirically rejected — its forward chain points to logarithms and scientific notation. The verified bottleneck is 7.EE.A.2, with a fan-out of 4 into the SSE family.
Consolidation gradient grading criteria
Robust: correct across ≥ 2 independent items and conditions.
Emerging: correct under standard conditions.
Recognised but unstable: conceptual representation present but execution unstable.
Introduced not internalised: conceptual structure not yet formed.
Performance vs knowledge gap attribution
Four signals: (1) method visible on paper; (2) a similar item answered correctly earlier; (3) error pattern matches the cognitive-overload signature; (4) cross-test counter-evidence. The framework is based on Newman's (1977) error-analysis hierarchy.
Limitations of CCSS-Edexcel mapping
Bearings was initially mapped to 7.G.A.2, which the API showed to be inappropriate. Similar solids was initially mapped to HSG-MG.A.1, which the API also rejected. CCSS contains no standard that specifically covers these two Edexcel-only topics. Methodological implication: when Mindsweeper uses CCSS as the backbone, Edexcel-only topics should be marked as placeholders.
Scope and limitations
① The standards backbone is CCSS-M; the student's actual curriculum is Edexcel GCSE 9-1 Foundation.
② The standards progression chain reflects logical prerequisite relationships, not necessarily instructional order.
③ Trait and factor analyses are based on roughly four weeks of data and constitute working hypotheses rather than settled conclusions.
④ Performance-versus-knowledge attribution involves judgement; every attribution is accompanied by explicit evidence.
⑤ LVN strategies have population-level research support; individual responses vary.
